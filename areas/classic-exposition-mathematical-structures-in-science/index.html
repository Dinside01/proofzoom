This area contains historically important expository essays that reveal how mathematical structures clarify scientific ideas.

The emphasis is not on popular science summary, experimental detail, or technical machinery for its own sake. The emphasis is on the mathematical ideas that make the science intelligible: coordinates, reference frames, invariance, symmetry, transformation, geometry, curvature, probability, dynamics, and related structures.

Entries in this area may be republications, annotated classics, or carefully curated expository pieces. They are included because they display unusually clear mathematical explanation.

ProofZoom remains a site devoted to mathematical clarity. This area recognizes that some of the most illuminating examples of mathematical thought arise when mathematics gives structure to science.
